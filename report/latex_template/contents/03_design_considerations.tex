\section{Design Considerations}
\label{sec:design_considerations}

This section explains how the target specifications are translated into circuit
requirements. Include first-order calculations, design constraints, and the main
trade-offs that guide the implementation.

\subsection{Requirement Derivation}

Derive the minimum requirements for the most critical circuit parameters. Typical
examples include gain, bandwidth, noise, settling time, slew rate, output swing,
matching, input common-mode range, or loading.

\begin{equation}
    \text{Required parameter} = \text{function of target specifications}.
\end{equation}

State the final requirement clearly after each derivation.

\subsection{Design Trade-Offs}

Discuss the trade-offs that shape the circuit choices. Focus on constraints that
meaningfully affect the final design, such as speed versus power, gain versus
bandwidth, device size versus parasitic capacitance, and accuracy versus area.

\subsection{Verification Plan}

Summarize the simulations needed to verify the design. Include the testbench
condition, process corner, temperature, supply voltage, input condition, and
metric extraction method when applicable.
